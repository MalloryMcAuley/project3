My Favorite City: Westport, Ireland

My favorite city is Westport, Ireland. I have been here three times now because it is where my dad was born and many of his family members still live. I love this place for many reasons, such as the music, the pubs, and the just how beautiful the nature is. However, my favorite part about Westport is the people.

About Westport, Ireland

Location: Country Mayo, on the coast on Ireland between Clew Bay and Croagh Patrick
Town Population: ~6,000 people
Climate: Oceanic / maritime (mild and wet), in summer about 15–20°C and in winter about 3–8°C
Language: Primarily English with Irish (Gaeilge), occasionally used in signs and cultural events

The Pubs

Pubs in Ireland are the cornerstone of social life. They are more like community centers where people go to gather, socialize, celebrate, and as a bonus drink alchohol. My Irish family loves to settle into a pub into the late hours of the night to tell stories, laugh, drink way too much, and by the end of the night when everyone has hit, what I like to call "pub-chosis", they break out into song. I know this sounds like the ultimate cliche of an Irish family, but it's true.

Westport's Best Pubs

  • Toby's Bar; This is owned by one of my dads childhood friends so of course I had to put it first
  • Matt Molloy's; Live traditional music almost every night Official Website
  • J McGing; Traditional, welcoming, and lively, the way a pub should be.

The Nature

Westport, Ireland is one of the most beautiful places to spend time in nature. It sits between Clew Bay and the holiest mountian in Ireland, Mount Croagh Patrick. It is rugged coastlines mixed with rolling green hills, water vs mountians, making it feel truly magical.

Best Scenic Spots

  • Mount Croagh Patrick; While this is a stunning hike, it is 2 miles of intense elevation. But, the view from the top is amazing More Info
  • Berta Beach; A beautiful sandy beach to have a picnic on a sunny day.
  • Clew Bay; Stunning views of the islands and adjacent to the historic center.

The Music

The music scene in Wesport is lively with almost nightly live music. The pubs are filled with traditional Irish sessions that get everyone up and dancing. If you're lucky, the whole pub will sing along.

Historic Westport House

The Historic Westport House is one of Ireland's most iconic estates. It was built in the 18th century on the cite of a medieval castle. Originally it was the family home of the Browne family, who are said to be descendants of Grace O’Malley. She was a famous pirate queen between 1530 and 1603. This connects Westport House to Ireland’s rich seafaring and cultural history. Today, Westport House serves as both a museum and a cultural attraction, offering guided tours of the grand rooms, art collections, and beautifully landscaped gardens.
